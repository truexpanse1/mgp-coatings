# 5 Questions to Ask Any Pool Deck Contractor Before You Sign

**The exact questions that separate real pros from part-time hustlers**

Pool decks fail faster than almost any coated surface we work on. Constant water, chlorine, UV, and foot traffic all at once. A mediocre install shows its cracks inside a single summer.

Here are the 5 questions every contractor hopes you won't ask. Ask all 5. Take notes. Compare answers. The right contractor will thank you for asking — the wrong one will get defensive.

---

## Question 1: "How does your coating handle slip resistance when wet, and what data supports that?"

**Why it matters:** A pool deck is always wet. The whole point of coating it is safer footing than bare concrete. But "slip-resistant" is marketing fluff without a coefficient-of-friction rating.

**The right answer:** A named texturing method (broadcast sand, quartz, or aluminum oxide), a data sheet you can look at, and honesty that *any* coated surface can be slippery when wet — but textured systems meaningfully outperform bare concrete or smooth paint.

**Red flag:** "Oh yeah, it's totally non-slip." No coating is 100% slip-proof. A contractor who won't admit that is selling.

---

## Question 2: "Is your top coat specifically formulated for chlorine and UV exposure? Can I see the spec sheet?"

**Why it matters:** Generic concrete sealers and driveway epoxies aren't built for pool environments. Chlorine, muriatic acid, and direct sun all break down coatings that aren't specced for it. When the coating fails, the concrete underneath starts deteriorating too.

**The right answer:** A named polyaspartic or polyurethane topcoat with a published spec sheet showing chlorine resistance and UV stability. A good contractor will hand you the manufacturer's data sheet without hesitation.

**Red flag:** "It's a standard 2-part epoxy." Straight epoxy without UV stabilizers will yellow and chalk within a year in California sun.

---

## Question 3: "Walk me through your prep process step by step. What happens before the first coat?"

**Why it matters:** This is the one that separates the pros from the weekend warriors. The failure rate of pool deck coatings is overwhelmingly a prep problem, not a product problem.

**The right answer:** Pressure-washing or diamond grinding depending on the existing surface, crack repair with structural epoxy, dropped grout lines (if tile), moisture testing, full vacuum-down, and masking of coping, tile, and pool equipment.

**Red flag:** "We just power-wash and start coating." That's a 2-year coating at best.

---

## Question 4: "What's your warranty, and what exactly does it cover?"

**Why it matters:** A verbal "we stand behind our work" is worth nothing when the phone stops being returned. A manufacturer-only warranty covers product defects but not installation failures — which is where 85% of problems come from.

**The right answer:** A written warranty of 10+ years (15+ is the pro standard) covering BOTH material AND workmanship, honored directly by the contractor who installed the job.

**Red flag:** "The manufacturer warrants the product for life." Doesn't matter if the product is fine but the installer botched adhesion — nobody's coming back to fix it.

---

## Question 5: "Can you show me 3 pool decks you've done locally, and let me call those homeowners?"

**Why it matters:** Pool deck work is specialized. A contractor who mostly does garage floors or driveways can sell you a pool deck — but they may not have actually done many. Stock before/after photos don't prove anything.

**The right answer:** Three real local addresses with real homeowners willing to talk. A contractor who's done good work gets excited by this request. They've built a reputation they want you to verify.

**Red flag:** "We keep our clients' information private" or "let me pull up some photos online." That's a dodge.

---

## Bonus: The two-sentence gut check

At the end of every pool deck consultation, ask this:

> *"What's the one thing about this job that would make you turn it down or walk away?"*

A real pro will give you a specific answer — maybe it's concrete that's too far gone, maybe it's a homeowner with unrealistic timing, maybe it's a job too small to be worth the mobilization.

A hustler will say "nothing, we can handle anything." That tells you they'll take on jobs they shouldn't — and yours may be one of them.

---

## Bonus bonus: Your own pre-quote checklist

Before any contractor arrives, take 5 minutes and note:

- [ ] Is the concrete cracked? Where, how wide?
- [ ] Are there spalled (flaking) areas?
- [ ] Is there efflorescence (white chalky residue)?
- [ ] Any previous coating or sealer visible?
- [ ] Is the deck exposed to afternoon sun, morning sun, or shaded?
- [ ] How old is the pool? (Age affects chemistry exposure history)

The contractor who asks about all of these before quoting is already a level above the one who doesn't.
