Why we made this

You've seen a thousand "before and after" driveway photos online. Most of them are stock. None of them come with actual costs, timelines, or the story of what went wrong before we got there.

This is the opposite. Twelve real driveways, Central Coast addresses (lightly redacted for privacy), real numbers, and Matt's honest notes on each one.

Project 01 — Templeton

950 sqft · $4,800 · 2 days

Before: 15-year-old broom-finish concrete with oil staining in three spots, two hairline cracks, heavy efflorescence near the garage door.

What we did: Diamond grind, structural epoxy crack repair, 100% solids primer, broadcast chip (Silverstone), polyaspartic topcoat.

"Classic Templeton driveway. The efflorescence told us the concrete was wicking moisture — we did an extended moisture test to make sure we weren't locking water in under the coating. Homeowner texted me six months later to say the coating makes the whole house look 10 years newer."

Project 02 — Paso Robles

1,200 sqft · $6,400 · 1 day

Before: Exposed aggregate driveway with heavy fading from UV and minor surface spalling near the sidewalk.

What we did: Pressure wash, surface stabilization primer, color-tinted polyaspartic (tan with dark flake), satin finish.

"Exposed aggregate is tricky — you can't grind it flat without destroying the texture the homeowner paid for 20 years ago. We used a penetrating primer that locks the existing surface and gives the polyaspartic something to grab."

Project 03 — San Luis Obispo

780 sqft · $4,200 · 2 days

Before: Someone had tried to "seal" it with a clear acrylic 4 years ago. It had peeled in patches exposing contaminated concrete beneath.

What we did: Full chemical strip, diamond grind, spot moisture test, full system install with polyaspartic topcoat.

"This is the kind of job that's why I say 'cheap now = expensive later.' The acrylic sealer job was $800 four years ago. The strip-and-redo cost $1,400 more than it would have if they'd done it right the first time."

Project 04 — Atascadero

1,450 sqft · $7,900 · 2 days

Before: Cracked concrete with a settlement issue near the street, heavy tire marks, homeowner wanted match to interior garage coating.

What we did: Crack chase and epoxy repair, color-matched polyaspartic (dark gray with copper flake), sealed to meet the interior garage at the threshold.

"Matching an exterior driveway to an interior garage floor is a signature move. The homeowner said it's the first thing people comment on when they pull in."

Project 05 — Pismo Beach

620 sqft · $3,600 · 1 day

Before: Short coastal driveway with salt-air damage and a crumbling edge along the street transition.

What we did: Edge repair with structural mortar, diamond grind, epoxy primer, polyaspartic topcoat with fine aluminum oxide broadcast for grip.

"Salt air is relentless. We spec polyaspartic on every coastal job because it handles salt, UV, and moisture cycling better than anything else."

Project 06 — Cambria

1,100 sqft · $6,100 · 2 days

Before: Curved driveway climbing a hill, severe UV damage, previous contractor's coating had failed after 18 months.

What we did: Removed failed coating mechanically, ground concrete, full crack repair pass, two-coat polyaspartic system with textured broadcast for slope grip.

"Slope + wet conditions = grip is not optional. Broadcast heavier than standard and locked it in with a second polyaspartic coat. Homeowner drives a sports car and said the traction is 'like new concrete, but nicer looking.'"

Which finish adds the most curb appeal?

Of the 12 projects here, the ones homeowners reported the strongest reactions to were the dark gray polyaspartic with metallic or flake broadcast. It reads as modern, high-contrast against most home exteriors, and hides future dirt/tire marks better than lighter colors.

One thing Matt would do differently

On about 2 out of 12 jobs, Matt wishes he'd pushed harder on extending the coating under the garage door threshold.

Practical lesson for you: If you're considering both a garage floor AND a driveway coating, do them together. The continuous install is cleaner and the combined quote is almost always better than two separate ones.
